Information Processes and Technology

Description

Information Processes and Technology is the study of computer-based information systems. It focuses on information processes performed by these systems and the information technology that allows them to take place. Social, ethical and non-computer procedures resulting from the processes are considered. Different types of information systems are studied. Through project work, students will create their own information system to meet an identified need.

Is this for me?

The course teaches students about information-based systems (mostly systems involving computers) and covers the processes of collecting, organising, communicating and managing information, as well as the technologies which support them. With this knowledge, understanding and skill, students will be well placed to adapt to new technologies as they emerge. Students will appreciate the nature of information, its ethical use and its impact on many aspects of life. As such, they will be well prepared to pursue further education and employment across a particularly wide range of contexts relating to information technology. This course is structured so that 60% is theory-based with the remaining 40% being practical-based

Prerequisties: None. However, the study of Information & Software Technology in Years 9 & 10, is an advantage.

Topics Covered

Preliminary

Introduction to Information Skills and Systems - nature of data & information; social & ethical issues; information processes.
Tools for Information Processes - collecting;organising; analysing; processing; storing; transmitting; displaying.
Developing Information Systems using knowledge developed in previous units.
Students will be required to complete a group and personal system project.
Students will develop skills in spreadsheets, databases, graphics and website development.

HSC

Information Systems and Databases - organisation; storage & retrieval of data; related issues; making databases.
Communication systems - characteristics; transmitting & receiving; managing; related issues.
Project Management (students to design their own information computer system)
