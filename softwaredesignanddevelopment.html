Software Design and Development

Description

The Preliminary course introduces students to the basic concepts of computer software design and development. It does this by looking at the different ways in which software can be developed, the tools that can be used to assist in this process and by considering the interaction between software and the other components of the computer system. The HSC course builds on the Preliminary course and involves the development and documentation of software using a variety of data structures and language facilities. Students learn to solve a number of interesting and relevant software problems.

Is this for me?

The course teaches students about the processes which are involved in problem solving, offering students the opportunity to be creative, excellent career prospects and interesting content. In the course students will examine the many different approaches that can be taken to develop software. The many skills which are learnt throughout the course will be complimented by a major Software Design project, due Year 12, Term 3. This course is about developing rather than using software and therefore focuses significant amounts of time on the theoretical aspects of software development. Students who perform well in this subject demonstrate analytical thinking.

Prerequisties: None. However, the study of Information & Software Technology in Years 9 & 10 and Level 5.3 Mathematics is an advantage.
